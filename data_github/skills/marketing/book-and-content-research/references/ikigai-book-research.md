## The 10 Rules of Ikigai
1. Stay active and don't retire
2. Take it slow
3. Don't fill your stomach
4. Surround yourself with good friends
5. Get in shape for your next birthday
6. Smile
7. Reconnect with nature
8. Give thanks
9. Live in the moment
10. Follow your ikigai

## Author Backstory
- **Héctor García** (born 1981, Valencia, Spain) — software engineer, studied at ETH Zurich, moved to Japan 2004. Runs Kirainet.com blog. Pen name: "Kirai". Observed elderly Japanese neighbours' remarkable vitality → became fascinated with ikigai.
- **Francesc Miralles** (born 1968, Barcelona) — 30+ books on philosophy/spirituality/well-being. Deep passion for Eastern culture and Buddhism.
- **How the book was born**: Met at a party in Tokyo. García told Miralles about ikigai and Okinawa centenarians. Both instantly connected. Traveled to Ogimi village, Okinawa — highest concentration of 100+ year-olds in the world — and conducted in-person interviews.
- García quote: *"We met people in their 90s and 100s still gardening, singing, dancing. They all had one thing in common: a clear reason to live — their ikigai."*
