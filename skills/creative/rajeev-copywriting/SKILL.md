# Rajeev's Copywriting Skill

## About the Author
- Name: Rajeev
- Role: Data support and copywriting professional
- Audience: Businesses (B2B)

## Brand Voice Guidelines

### Tone
- Professional but friendly — not stiff or corporate, not overly casual
- Warm and approachable, while maintaining credibility
- Confident without being arrogant
- Clear and direct — no fluff or filler

### Style
- Write in first person when drafting for Rajeev
- Use plain, jargon-free language unless the audience is technical
- Short sentences preferred for clarity
- Active voice over passive voice
- End with a clear call to action where appropriate

### Audience
- Businesses (B2B) — decision-makers, managers, and teams
- Assume the reader is busy — get to the point quickly
- Lead with value or the problem being solved
- Speak to outcomes and results, not just features

## Content Types
When asked to write copy, confirm or infer the format:
- LinkedIn posts
- Email outreach / newsletters
- Website copy / landing pages
- Service descriptions
- Social media captions
- Case study summaries

## Process
1. Clarify the goal: what action should the reader take?
2. Clarify the format if not specified
3. Draft using the brand voice above
4. Keep paragraphs short (2-3 lines max for digital content)
5. Offer 1-2 variations if the brief is short or open-ended

## Example Phrases That Fit the Voice
- "Here's how we help businesses like yours..."
- "Let's make this simple."
- "We get it — [pain point]. That's why..."
- "Ready to [outcome]? Let's talk."

## Pitfalls to Avoid
- Avoid buzzwords: synergy, leverage, disruptive, holistic
- No overly salesy language ("Act now! Limited time!")
- Don't use passive voice: "It was decided" → "We decided"
- Don't write walls of text — break it up
